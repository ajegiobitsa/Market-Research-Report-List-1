<p><p>In the microscopy market, camera applications predominantly feature CCD (Charge-Coupled Device) and CMOS (Complementary Metal-Oxide-Semiconductor) technologies. CCD cameras are known for their high sensitivity and image quality, making them suitable for low-light imaging and scientific research. Conversely, CMOS cameras offer faster processing speeds, lower power consumption, and integrated electronics, which facilitate compact designs. Both camera types cater to diverse microscopy needs, driving advancements in fields such as biological research, materials science, and quality control in various industries.</p></p>
