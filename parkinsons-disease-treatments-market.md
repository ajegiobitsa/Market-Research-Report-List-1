<p><p>The Parkinson's Disease treatments market consists of several drug categories. Dopamine agonists stimulate dopamine receptors, while Levodopa replenishes dopamine levels. Glutamate antagonists help modulate excitatory neurotransmission. Apomorphine offers fast relief from symptoms by acting on dopamine receptors. COMT inhibitors extend the effects of Levodopa, and anticholinergics reduce tremors by blocking neurotransmitter action. MAO-B inhibitors slow dopamine breakdown, enhancing its availability. Other drugs encompass various therapies addressing symptoms or underlying mechanisms of Parkinson's Disease.</p></p>
