<p><p>Allergy immunotherapy is a treatment aimed at desensitizing the immune system to specific allergens. Subcutaneous immunotherapy (SCIT) involves injecting allergens under the skin, gradually increasing dosages to build tolerance. This method is highly effective for various allergies but requires regular visits to a healthcare provider. Sublingual immunotherapy (SLIT), on the other hand, involves placing allergen tablets or drops under the tongue, offering a more convenient, at-home alternative. Both methods aim to reduce allergy symptoms and improve quality of life.</p></p>
