<p><p>Hollow fiber filtration membranes are categorized into microfiltration and ultrafiltration types. Microfiltration membranes feature larger pore sizes, typically in the range of 0.1 to 10 microns, effectively removing suspended solids, bacteria, and certain larger viruses from liquids. Ultrafiltration membranes have smaller pores, ranging from 1 nanometer to 0.1 microns, enabling the separation of smaller particles, including macromolecules and some viruses. Both types are widely used in water treatment, food processing, and biopharmaceutical industries due to their efficiency and scalability.</p></p>
