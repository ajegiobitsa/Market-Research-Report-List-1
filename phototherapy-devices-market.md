Conventional phototherapy devices utilize fluorescent or LED lights to deliver therapeutic wavelengths for conditions like neonatal jaundice and skin disorders. In contrast, fiber optic phototherapy devices employ fibers to emit light, allowing for a more targeted approach, especially in pediatric applications. Both types aim to improve patient outcomes by providing effective light treatment options tailored to specific medical needs.</p></p>
